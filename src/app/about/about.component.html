About

I am a recent alumni of the computer programming bootcamp Epicodus in Portland, Oregon. I took the Java/Android track, consisting of four five-week sections and a five-week internship.

Epicodus was a great experience. I already had some exposure to coding going in, having developed a few Android apps, but they were able to give me a solid foundation for a career as a developer. I learned programming fundamentals, industry-standard tools like Git and Node, and a number of other tools and frameworks.

Aside from the technical, Epicodus also helped me hone my interpersonal skills. Every day we paired up with another student, going through the days lessons on a shared computer. Pair programming allows you to have two minds working on the same problem, but it can also be a challenge. You have to be mindful of the time and space that you take up, and make sure that your partner feels comfortable speaking their mind, sharing ideas, and asking for a turn at the keyboard.

I interned at EyeCue Lab in downtown Portland. They are a small agency of about ten people who do really great work. I started off doing some manual QA on one of their larger projects, Powur, finding and reporting bugs. After a few weeks, I started taking on tasks myself. I improved the responsiveness of components, added CSS animations, and made decisions about the UI. After five weeks, I had gained some momentum and was sad it had to end.

I am currently looking for work, so feel free to contact me about any job opportunities!
